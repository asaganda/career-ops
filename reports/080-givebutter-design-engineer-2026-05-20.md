## C) Level & Strategy

**Detected level:** Mid-Senior IC, but filtered first on design identity — not years. The VP of Design reviews all applicants. This is not a standard frontend hire with design elements bolted on. It is a design engineering role where craft, motion, and taste are the primary deliverables.

**Andrew's natural level:** Mid-Senior frontend engineer with strong functional e-commerce background. That background is real and deep — but it doesn't align with what this role is buying.

**The core mismatch:** The JD describes the role as existing for "engineers who miss designing or designers who learned to code." Andrew is neither. He is a frontend engineer who has built functional, high-traffic UIs — but his portfolio is conversion-optimized e-commerce, not motion-forward, design-crafted marketing surfaces. The VP of Design screening makes this a design portfolio review first, a code review second.

**If the animation gap weren't there:** The Figma and design taste gaps would still be blockers. This role requires someone who lives at the design/engineering intersection and can push back on design direction with aesthetic judgment. There's no evidence in Andrew's background to demonstrate that.

**Recommendation:** Do not apply. Three simultaneous hard blockers (motion/animation, Figma, design taste) map to the core daily function of this job. No framing strategy can bridge them.
