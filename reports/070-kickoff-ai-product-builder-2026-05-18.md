**Detected level:** Mid-to-senior IC, product-builder archetype — defined entirely by output quality and speed, not title or years

**Andrew's natural level:** Good match for the builder framing. TypescriptQuest (idea → production) and CanIParkHere (prototype in days) are exactly what this role calls for. The gap is that Kickoff wants someone who also does formal user research, writes structured requirements, and owns launch metrics — Andrew hasn't done this in a formal team context.

**Key nuance:** This role is meaningfully more PM-heavy than eBay Live. eBay wanted someone who ships 24-hour prototypes. Kickoff wants someone who owns a product area through its entire lifecycle, which includes discovery, user interviews, success metrics, and QA — not just fast prototyping. TypescriptQuest is proof of the full cycle in a solo context.

**Sell it:**
- TypescriptQuest covers solo product lifecycle end-to-end: identified the gap in TypeScript learning tools → scoped the MVP → built with Claude Code → launched with Stripe + OAuth → real users.
- CanIParkHere shows the rapid prototype-to-validation loop: pain point → working prototype with GPT-4.1 vision → user testing in the field.
- Conntext shows shipping to a real distribution channel (Chrome Web Store) with real users — product sense beyond just prototyping.
- All three used AI coding agents as the primary build layer — this is exactly the "your superpower is shipping fast using AI" framing.

**If downleveled:** This role doesn't use traditional levels. Accept if comp is fair; negotiate a 90-day check-in on product area ownership scope.
